---
title: Peter Bergmann - The man who ceased to exist
tags: bio life personal
---

The most impressive thing about Mr. Bergmann is that this, probably, is
not even his name. The man was found dead at Rosses Point beach as if his body,
wearing shorts and a T-shirt, was washed back to the sand by the sea. The rest
of his clothes were found nearby, hidden.

There was no identification. The man was traced by several cameras to a
hotel where he stayed, casually strolling through the city for several moments and
alone. Mr. Bergmann paid in cash, used a fake name and was recorded by the
cameras leaving his room every day with a small bag, probably containing his personal
belongings, that he gradually threw away until he had nothing left. Even his
clothes had no tags (purposely removed).

It turns out that Mr. Bergmann had terminal prostate cancer and weeks to
live. He decided to die alone, looking at a beautiful view and just vanish
without a trace. Ironically, the man died from a heart attack after barely entering
the water and, to this day, nobody knows who he was.

Don’t you also, sometimes, just wish that you could be as free as
someone that leaves on his own terms? I know I wish.
