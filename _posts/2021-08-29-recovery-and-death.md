---
title: About recovery and death
tags: sickness covid
category: English Diary
---

As I recover my aunt is there, hanging between life and death, also due to COVID. It is sometimes hard to concentrate because I’m thinking of her. She is the sweetest woman and surely deserved a better end than to die alone, unconscious in a hospital bed and full of tubes. At this very moment she is in the deepest coma possible and unresponsive to any kind of stimulus. All I can do is hope for the best as her body wants to die but medical science keeps it alive.

I am always thinking about what she is feeling. Is she dreaming? In a kind of limbo full of darkness waiting for some sort of light to shine and guide her someplace? Or is she already gone and all that is left is an empty husk that the machines are nurturing?

It is sad to think that there is no reasoning in dying: we just do. Maybe on a Sunday while the rest of the family is having lunch or on a Wednesday when the telenovela is playing. Someone’s light is always fading, and someone’s light is always igniting.
